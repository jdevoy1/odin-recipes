Mac and Cheese

Description

This is a simple baked mac and cheese recipe by Chef John. It uses cheddar cheese and panko to form a crispy crust, and includes freshly ground nutmeg, cayenne, and the unpronounceable Worcestershire sauce. Dijon mustard is used in place of dry mustard. The end result is a filling, savory, and delicious mac and cheese dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 (16 ounce) package elbow macaroni
  • ¼ cup butter
  • ¼ cup all-purpose flour
  • ¼ teaspoon dried thyme
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • ⅛ teaspoon white pepper
  • 3 cups milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  • 3 cups shredded sharp Cheddar cheese, divided
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • ½ cup panko bread crumbs
  • 1 tablespoon butter, melted

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F (200 degrees C).
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook elbow macaroni in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 8 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  3. Melt 1/4 cup butter in a large saucepan over medium heat. When butter starts to foam and bubble, stir in flour; cook on medium heat until flour begins to turn pale yellow, 3 to 4 minutes. Add thyme, cayenne, and white pepper; cook and stir another minute, then whisk in 1 cup milk until smooth. Pour in remaining milk and whisk again. Bring sauce just to a simmer.
  4. Stir in salt, Worcestershire sauce, and nutmeg. Simmer, whisking often, on medium-low heat until thickened, about 8 minutes.
  5. Turn heat off, then add 2 1/4 cups Cheddar cheese; stir until melted and combined. Mix in Dijon mustard.
  6. Transfer macaroni into a casserole dish, then pour in cheese sauce; stir to thoroughly combine pasta with sauce.
  7. Mix together panko bread crumbs and 1 tablespoon melted butter in a small bowl. Sprinkle mixture over macaroni and cheese. Sprinkle remaining 3/4 cup Cheddar cheese on top.
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until top is golden brown, about 20 minutes.
